About us

A proper Cornish swim school, run by people who love teaching it

We've been teaching Cornwall to swim since 2002. From a baby's very first time in the water to adults learning later in life, there's a class here for every age and ability — beginners, nervous swimmers, and those who just want to sharpen their technique.

We're based in St Columb Major, an easy drive from Newquay, Truro, Bodmin, Wadebridge and everywhere in between. Lessons are competitively priced and come in a few shapes: one-to-one, shared private, or groups.

Fun and a friendly atmosphere sit at the heart of it — wherever you are on your swimming journey, you're welcome in the water.

Lessons

From first splashes to racing lengths

Children follow the Swim England certificate ladder, with our own Fitness 4 U awards in between — so every swimmer takes home a certificate twice a year, whatever stage they're at.

0–2 yrs

Parent & Baby / Toddler

Gentle, playful classes built around nursery rhymes and water games. Little ones build confidence with a parent beside them — learning that water is fun before any fear can creep in.

3 yrs +

Swim School

Children move up on their own once they turn three, slotted into the right group for their ability. Lessons are fun first, but always teaching proper technique — and there's expert support for nervous swimmers and additional needs.

One-to-one

1:1 & 1:2 Lessons

Focused sessions shaped entirely around your child, ironing out the weak spots to build a balanced swimmer. Available from age three — intensive 15-minute slots, or 30 minutes if you'd prefer.

Adults

Adult Lessons

It's never too late. Whether you've never swum, lost your nerve in deep water, or want a stronger stroke, our adult classes meet you where you are — we've taught plenty of swimmers in their 60s and 70s.

Invitation

Swim Academy

Introduced in 2006, our top swimmers are invited to step up from learn to swim into an introduction to competitive swimming — technique, stamina and racing, to see who's got real promise in the pool.

Twice a year

Our Swim Galas

Every swimmer is invited — there are races for all abilities, right down to non-swimmers. Loud cheers all round, and a medal for every single competitor, not just the podium.

Aquafit

A proper workout if you put the effort in — and there's nowhere better to be put through your paces. You don't need to swim a stroke; every class is in standing-depth water.

The water takes the load off your joints, so impact stays low while you work. Think of it as an all-over jacuzzi with an aerobic workout thrown in.

Great for anyone managing joint problems.

Children's Triathlon

Our junior tri team has been taking on local events since summer 2019 — all about fun and taking part, with a medal for every finisher.

There's a scootathlon for ages 5–8 and mini triathlons for 8–16, with a Fitness 4 U team cheering them on. Fancy giving it a go? Everyone's welcome.

Good to know

Questions parents ask

A few of the most common ones. There's more we're always happy to talk through — just ask.

Does my child need armbands?

No. If they'd like to wear them at first that's absolutely fine — we'll work with them and take the armbands off as soon as they're ready.

Should my child eat before swimming?

Please don't eat within an hour of swimming. Eating any later than that can bring on cramp or sickness in the water.

Can we take photos or videos?

Not during lessons, for child safeguarding. We can arrange a dedicated photo session though — including underwater shots and video. Just ask at reception.

Should I take my child swimming between lessons?

Yes — the more time they spend in the water, the quicker their confidence builds and the faster they improve.

How long will it take my child to learn?

Every child is different. Little ones who've come since babyhood often swim unaided around two. Most can manage 10m on their front and back by about five with regular lessons.

My child is nervous about coming. What should I do?

Let us know — it's really common, even well into lessons. Perseverance works best, and it helps us to know what they're finding tricky. Everything's treated in the strictest confidence.
