About us

Far more than a swim school

Fitness 4 U started life as a swim school in 2002. We still teach hundreds of children to swim every week, but the pool does plenty more these days: aquatic fitness and rehab classes, chair-based exercise, sports massage, and a sensory pool you can book privately. There's a pool hoist and an adult changing table, so the pool is genuinely open to everyone, including less mobile and immobile clients.

You'll find us on the Lanvale industrial estate just outside St Columb Major, a short drive from Newquay, Truro, Bodmin and Wadebridge. Classes are kept small, usually around six children to a group, and a lot of our swimmers were nervous about getting in at first. Nobody gets rushed.

The school is run by Jan, who has taught swimming in Cornwall for over twenty years, with a team who tend to know the families they teach by name.

Good to know

Questions parents ask

A few of the ones we get asked most. If yours isn't here, just ask.

Does my child need armbands?

No. If they'd like to wear them at first that's absolutely fine. We'll work with them and take the armbands off as soon as they're ready.

Should my child eat before swimming?

Please don't eat within an hour of swimming. Eating any later than that can bring on cramp or sickness in the water.

Can we take photos or videos?

Not during lessons, for child safeguarding. We can arrange a dedicated photo session though, including underwater shots and video. Just ask at reception.

Should I take my child swimming between lessons?

Yes. The more time they spend in the water, the quicker their confidence builds and the faster they improve.

How long will it take my child to learn?

Every child is different. Little ones who've come since babyhood often swim unaided around two, and most can manage 10m on their front and back by about five with regular lessons.

My child is nervous about coming. What should I do?

Let us know. It's really common, even well into lessons, and it helps us to know what they're finding tricky. Anything you tell us stays between us.
